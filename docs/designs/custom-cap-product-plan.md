# Custom Cap Product Plan

## Product Goal

Create a small first range of caps that can be sampled, photographed, shown for feedback, and possibly launched in a small quantity.

Do not start with a huge product range. The first goal is to learn what people like.

## Cap Types To Consider

### Snapbacks

- Structured front.
- Adjustable plastic snap at the back.
- Popular for streetwear and bold designs.
- Good for front embroidery.

### Trucker Caps

- Foam or structured front with mesh back.
- Casual, sporty, and summer-friendly.
- Good for bold front logos or text.

### Dad Caps

- Softer, curved brim, relaxed fit.
- Good for minimalist embroidery.
- Easy everyday style.

### Fitted Caps

- No adjustable strap.
- Need exact sizes.
- Harder for a beginner because stock planning is more complicated.

### Beanies Later

- Good winter product.
- Easier sizing.
- Best as a later expansion after caps.

## Embroidery vs Printed Designs

### Embroidery

Best for:

- Text
- Simple logos
- Initials
- Small icons
- Premium feel

Watch-outs:

- Tiny details may not stitch clearly.
- More colours can cost more.
- Thick designs may feel stiff.

### Printing

Best for:

- Larger graphics
- Flat artwork
- Colourful designs
- Lower-cost tests in some cases

Watch-outs:

- May feel less premium on caps.
- Durability depends on the method and supplier.
- Not every cap material prints well.

Beginner recommendation: start with embroidery for the first cap samples unless a supplier clearly shows high-quality printed caps.

## Simple First Product Range

Pick 3 to 5 concepts for samples or mockups.

1. Minimal Logo Cap
   - Small logo or wordmark on the front.
   - Dad cap or snapback.
   - Black, navy, or stone.

2. Bold Text Cap
   - One short word or brand name across the front.
   - Snapback or trucker cap.
   - Black cap with white embroidery.

3. Side Detail Cap
   - Small front mark plus side embroidery.
   - Dad cap or snapback.
   - Neutral cap with one accent colour.

4. Custom Initial Cap
   - Initials, name, or number.
   - Good for testing personalization.
   - Keep the design simple.

5. Limited Colour Drop
   - Same design on 2 or 3 colourways.
   - Use polls to choose the best colour before ordering.

## Starter Colours

Safe starting colours:

- Black
- White
- Navy
- Grey
- Stone / cream

Accent colours:

- Red
- Royal blue
- Forest green
- Gold
- Light blue

Beginner recommendation: start with 2 or 3 cap colours only. Black with white embroidery is usually the safest test.

## Design Placement Options

- Front centre: main logo, text, or symbol.
- Front left: smaller premium-feel mark.
- Side: small icon, initials, or short detail.
- Back: website, slogan, small logo, or custom text.
- Under brim: cool detail, but often more expensive and less visible.

Beginner recommendation: start with front embroidery only, then add side or back details later.

## Information Needed Before Ordering Samples

Before ordering samples, collect:

- Chosen cap type.
- Cap colour.
- Embroidery or print method.
- Design file format required by the supplier.
- Maximum embroidery size.
- Number of thread colours allowed.
- Sample price.
- Minimum order quantity after sample approval.
- Shipping cost and delivery time.
- Whether the supplier can digitize artwork for embroidery.
- Whether they show a proof before making the sample.
- Return or remake policy if quality is poor.
- Whether a parent or guardian must place the order.
