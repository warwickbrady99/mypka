# My Life

This is the part of my myPKA that holds the life concepts I actually work with. Read the shape correctly: there are **four buckets** — Topics, Habits, Projects, and Key Elements — plus **Goals, which are not a bucket but the operating layer** that sits on top of them. Each bucket has its own flat folder. Everything connects through Obsidian-style `[[wikilinks]]` rather than nested hierarchies.

## The four buckets and the Goals operating layer

- **Key Elements** are the load-bearing walls — the stable dimensions of a life (Health, Family, Career, Wealth). They change slowly.
- **Topics** are the signal layer — interest areas you follow before they are worth promoting.
- **Projects** are bounded efforts with a finish line.
- **Habits** are recurring rhythms with a cadence, no finish line.
- **Goals** sit above all four. A Goal is the operating layer: an outcome you are actively driving toward right now. It does not live in its own silo — it reaches down into the buckets and binds them together.

## The anchoring law

**Every Goal anchors to exactly one Key Element. Never to a Project. Never to a Topic.** The Key Element is the "why this matters" — the stable wall the Goal serves. If a Goal cannot name a single Key Element it belongs to, the Goal is either misframed or pointed at something that has not yet earned Key-Element status.

Example: the Goal *"lose 20 kg"* anchors to the Key Element [[health]]. Health is the wall; the Goal is the push against it.

## The carrier rule

A Goal is delivered by **one carrier — a Project OR a Habit, never both, never neither.** Projects and Habits are siblings here: two different shapes a carrier can take. A Project carries a Goal when the work has a finish line. A Habit carries it when the work is a rhythm with no end.

Example: *"lose 20 kg"* is carried **either** by a Project (an 8-week program with an end date) **or** by a Habit (3 workouts a week, ongoing) — pick one carrier. If you find yourself naming both, split the Goal or pick the dominant carrier.

## Topic to Key Element promotion (and the reverse archive)

A Topic is where a new interest lands first. When a Topic crystallizes into a measurable pursuit you intend to drive, it **graduates into a Key Element** — and only then can a Goal anchor to it.

Example: *French* starts as a Topic — something you are reading about and dabbling in. When it sharpens into a measurable pursuit ("reach B2 fluency"), it graduates into a Key Element. Only after that promotion can a Goal anchor to it.

The reverse also holds: when a Key Element goes cold — no Goals anchored to it, no active push — archive it back down rather than letting it pose as a wall.

## The filter test

Before filing anything in My Life, ask: **does this belong in My Life at all?** My Life holds the concepts you actively work with — the walls, the rhythms, the pushes, the signals. Reference facts, contacts, and documents live elsewhere (CRM, Documents). If a candidate is not a Topic, Habit, Project, Key Element, or Goal, it does not belong here.
