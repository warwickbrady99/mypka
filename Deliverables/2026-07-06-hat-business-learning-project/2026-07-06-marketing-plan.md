# Marketing Plan

## Goal

Sell a small first version of a local activewear hat brand.

Keep the marketing practical:

- Local first.
- Real conversations first.
- Simple content first.
- Prove demand before spending too much money.

## Target Customer

People nearby who are into:

- Gym.
- Running.
- Football.
- Sports clubs.
- Casual activewear.
- Local lifestyle and streetwear.
- Teen and young adult fashion.

## Simple Brand Message

Your marketing should say what it is, who it is for, and why it is wearable.

Clean activewear caps for local gym, running, football, and everyday sporty style.

## Dad / Adult Support

Your dad handles the adult side of selling.

Dad handles:

- Payment setup.
- Seller accounts.
- Supplier accounts.
- Official/legal/tax parts.
- Final approval before orders.

You handle:

- Design ideas.
- Research.
- Asking people what they would wear.
- Brand content ideas.
- Helping with product choices.

## Local Selling Channels

Choose 1 or 2 at first.

| Channel | Why it might work | What to do | Result |
|---|---|---|---|
| Friends and family | Fast honest feedback and first supporters | Show designs, ask if they would wear them |  |
| Local gym people | Direct target customer | Ask about colours, logo size, and price |  |
| Football teams | Local sports audience | Ask players what they would wear before/after training |  |
| Running groups | Good match for performance caps | Ask if breathable caps are useful |  |
| Instagram | Good for local style and photos | Post polls, mockups, samples, outfit shots |  |
| TikTok | Good for process and local brand story | Show design process, sample testing, first batch |  |
| Local events | Face-to-face feedback | Show samples or collect interest |  |

## Word Of Mouth Plan

Start small:

- Show 5 designs to people you trust.
- Ask them which one they would actually wear.
- Ask them who else locally might like it.
- Ask if they would share the page when it launches.
- Do not pressure people to buy.

## Instagram And TikTok Content Ideas

Simple post ideas:

- "Which colour should be first: black, grey, navy, white, or dark green?"
- "Small logo or bold logo?"
- "Would you wear this to the gym?"
- "Would you wear this for football training?"
- "Building a local activewear hat brand from scratch."
- Design sketch to mockup.
- Sample unboxing.
- Fit check with gym/casual outfit.
- Poll: "What price feels fair?"
- Behind the scenes: choosing the first supplier.

## Local Sports People

People to ask:

- Friends who go to the gym.
- Football players.
- Runners.
- Coaches.
- Sports club members.
- Older teens and young adults who dress sporty.

Ask:

- Would you wear this hat?
- What colour would you buy?
- Would you prefer a small logo or bold logo?
- Would you wear it for gym, running, football, or casual?
- What price would feel fair?
- Would you buy from a local young founder if the design was good?

## First Launch Plan

### Step 1: Design Feedback

Create 5 activewear hat design ideas.

Ask 10 to 20 local people:

- Which design they would actually wear.
- Which colour they would buy.
- Whether the logo should be small or bold.
- What price feels fair.

### Step 2: Interest List

Collect interest before spending too much.

Simple information to collect:

- First name.
- Contact method.
- Favourite design.
- Favourite colour.
- Whether they would buy if the sample looked good.

Dad should approve how contact details are collected and stored.

### Step 3: Choose Test Method

Decide with Dad:

- Pre-orders.
- Samples first.
- Small first batch.

Be honest with people about timing, price, and whether the product is still being tested.

## Basic Website Or Shop Plan

You may not need a full website immediately.

Possible order:

1. Instagram or TikTok page.
2. Interest form approved by Dad.
3. Simple product page if demand is real.
4. Bigger shop later.

First page should include:

- Brand name.
- Product photo or mockup.
- Colours.
- Price.
- Local pickup or shipping details.
- Contact or order method handled with Dad.

## Basic Metrics

Track these weekly:

| Metric | What it means | This week |
|---|---|---|
| Local people asked | Research effort |  |
| People who said yes/maybe | Early demand |  |
| Most popular design | Product direction |  |
| Most popular colour | Buying preference |  |
| Fair price answers | Pricing clue |  |
| Supplier sample cost | Feasibility |  |
| Followers or messages | Marketing interest |  |
| Pre-orders or committed buyers | Strong demand |  |

## Marketing Rules

- Keep the brand clean and wearable.
- Be honest that this is a new local brand.
- Do not copy logos or brand designs.
- Do not use school, team, or club logos without permission.
- Ask permission before posting someone's photo.
- Dad approves payments, official accounts, and customer-data handling.
