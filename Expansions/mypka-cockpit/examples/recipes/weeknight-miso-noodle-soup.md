# Weeknight Miso Noodle Soup

A fast end-of-day bowl from the pantry — about 20 minutes, light, and forgiving.
A study in [[japanese-cooking]] basics: build the broth, stage the vegetables,
finish with an egg.

## Ingredients

For 1 person:

- 1 portion ramen noodles
- 350-400 ml water + 1 tbsp miso paste
- 1-2 eggs
- 1-2 mushrooms, quartered
- 1 spring onion, sliced into rings
- chili oil to taste (optional)

## Method

1. Bring the water to a simmer, whisk in the miso paste off a rolling boil (so it
   keeps its aroma).
2. Add mushrooms for two minutes, then the noodles straight into the broth.
3. Crack the egg into the broth, cover, 3-4 minutes for a soft yolk.
4. Finish with spring onion and a few drops of chili oil.

## Notes

- Noodles cooked straight in the broth thicken it slightly — add 50-100 ml more
  water if you like it thinner.
- Scales cleanly to two: double broth, noodles, and egg.
