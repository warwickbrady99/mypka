## Decisions made

- **Question:** Where should the hat business project live?
  **Decision:** Put it in `Deliverables/2026-07-06-hat-business-learning-project/` because it is an active multi-file project, not a permanent PKM entity yet.

- **Question:** Should the plan make confident claims about suppliers, prices, and demand?
  **Decision:** No. The documents use research placeholders and exact questions because the user is new and needs to collect real information first.

- **Question:** Should the first business version be large or small?
  **Decision:** Keep the first version tiny: one niche, one hat type, a few designs, 10 to 20 customer conversations, and possibly one sample before any larger order.

- **Question:** Is this only a pretend learning project?
  **Decision:** No. It is a real selling project, but the first test stays small and Dad handles adult/legal/payment/supplier responsibilities.

- **Question:** What is the specific business direction?
  **Decision:** Focus on a local activewear hat brand for gym, running, football, sports clubs, casual activewear, local lifestyle/streetwear, and teen/young adult fashion.

## Insights

- The project should be treated as a learning business first, with low spending and real-world feedback before inventory.
- Because the user is 15, seller accounts, payments, purchases, public contact details, and customer data should involve a parent or guardian.
- Copyright and logo permissions are important early risks for a hat business, especially school, sports, brand, and character designs.
- The brand should feel local, sporty, clean, wearable, and serious enough that people would actually wear it for gym, football training, running, walking around town, or casual outfits.
