# Custom Cap Brand Launch Plan

## Principle

Start tiny, test quickly, and learn before spending much money.

## Phase 1: Idea And Brand Direction

Goal: decide the basic direction without overthinking.

Actions:

- Choose the main vibe: streetwear, sporty, luxury, funny, local, gaming, minimalist, or another clear style.
- Pick 3 possible brand names.
- Pick 3 possible slogans.
- Choose 2 or 3 starter colours.
- Decide whether the first designs use text, a logo, initials, symbols, or custom names.

Output:

- Updated `docs/brand/brand-foundation.md`.

## Phase 2: Research Suppliers

Goal: find safe and realistic sample options.

Actions:

- Use `docs/research/research-checklist.md`.
- Compare at least 3 suppliers.
- Check sample prices and minimum order quantities.
- Ask a parent or guardian to help with account and payment rules.

Output:

- A short supplier comparison table.

## Phase 3: Design First Cap Concepts

Goal: create 3 to 5 simple concepts.

Actions:

- Sketch ideas on paper or use a simple design tool.
- Keep designs readable and easy to embroider.
- Make basic mockups only for feedback.
- Avoid copyrighted designs.

Output:

- 3 to 5 cap concepts listed in `docs/designs/custom-cap-product-plan.md`.

## Phase 4: Order Samples

Goal: test real quality before selling.

Actions:

- Choose 1 to 3 sample designs.
- Get parent or guardian help with payment and ordering if needed.
- Ask supplier for a proof before production.
- Record total sample cost.

Output:

- Photos and notes for each sample.

## Phase 5: Get Feedback

Goal: learn what people actually like.

Actions:

- Ask friends, family, classmates, and followers.
- Use polls for colours and design choices.
- Ask if people would pay the likely price.
- Do not rely only on compliments; ask whether they would actually buy.

Output:

- Feedback notes and top design choice.

## Phase 6: Set Up Online Presence

Goal: create a simple place for people to follow the brand.

Actions:

- Make social media accounts with parent or guardian support where required.
- Post behind-the-scenes content.
- Share polls and sample updates.
- Research whether selling platforms allow under-18 owners or require adult help.

Output:

- Brand username list and first content plan.

## Phase 7: First Small Launch

Goal: sell a small amount without taking a big risk.

Actions:

- Launch one small batch or one pre-order test.
- Keep quantities low.
- Be clear about delivery times.
- Track every cost.
- Package orders neatly but cheaply.

Output:

- First launch results: units sold, revenue, costs, profit or loss, lessons.

## Phase 8: Review And Improve

Goal: decide what to do next based on evidence.

Actions:

- Review which designs got attention.
- Review what people bought.
- Review quality issues.
- Review pricing and profit.
- Decide whether to restock, redesign, or test a new cap.

Output:

- Next test plan.
