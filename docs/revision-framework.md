# Revision Framework

This file explains the study system. It turns confirmed course details from `docs/exam-board-map.md` into a practical weekly revision plan.

The goal is not to revise everything at once. The goal is to know what matters, what is weak, what comes next, and how AI tutoring should help.

## The Simple Loop

Use this loop for every subject.

1. Confirm the course details in `docs/exam-board-map.md`.
2. List the topics or modules.
3. Mark confidence for each topic.
4. Pick the next small revision task.
5. Practise with questions.
6. Review mistakes.
7. Ask AI for help in the right style.
8. Update the tracker.

## Confidence Scale

Use the same scale everywhere.

| Score | Meaning | What to do |
|---|---|---|
| 0 | I have not learned this yet | Ask for a simple explanation and examples |
| 1 | I recognise it but cannot do it alone | Learn the basics, then try guided questions |
| 2 | I can do easy questions | Practise mixed questions and fix mistakes |
| 3 | I can usually do it | Do exam-style practice |
| 4 | I am confident under timed conditions | Revisit later so it stays strong |

## What Counts As Evidence

Evidence means something real that shows how well the topic is going.

- A marked homework score.
- A test or mock result.
- A past-paper question.
- A teacher comment.
- A mistake you keep making.
- A topic you can explain clearly without notes.
- A tutoring session where you needed lots of help.

## What To Capture When You Struggle

Do not just write "bad at algebra" or "history is hard". Be specific enough that the next revision task is obvious.

Helpful struggle notes:

- I forget which formula to use.
- I understand the idea but lose marks because I do not show working.
- I know the facts but cannot structure the exam answer.
- I run out of time.
- I mix up two similar topics.
- I can do it in class but not in a test.
- I do not understand the vocabulary.

## How AI Tutoring Should Help

AI should help you learn, not skip the learning. Use it like a patient tutor.

Good AI tutoring should:

- Explain one idea at a time.
- Ask you questions before giving the full answer.
- Show worked examples when you are stuck.
- Give hints before solutions.
- Check your reasoning.
- Help turn mistakes into revision tasks.
- Create practice questions at the right level.
- Help with exam-answer structure.
- Adapt explanations to your course once the exam board is confirmed.

AI should not:

- Pretend an exam board is confirmed when it is not.
- Write assessed coursework for you.
- Give answers without helping you understand.
- Replace teacher instructions.
- Use random internet resources when the official specification is available.

## Tutoring Prompt Template

Use this prompt when asking for help.

```text
I am a student at Neston High School. I am revising <subject>.

Course details:
- Qualification:
- Exam board:
- Specification:
- Tier, if relevant:

Topic:

What I understand:

What I struggle with:

Please tutor me step by step. Ask me short questions, give hints before answers, and finish by giving me 3 practice questions matched to my level.
```

If the course is not confirmed yet, add this line:

```text
Some course details are still draft, so do not assume the exact exam board, route, tier, or specification unless I have listed it as confirmed.
```
