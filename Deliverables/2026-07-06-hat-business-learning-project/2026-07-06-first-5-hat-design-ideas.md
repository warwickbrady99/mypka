# First 5 Hat Design Ideas

## What To Do Next

Do not worry about suppliers, websites, tax, business registration, or big planning yet.

Your next job is simple:

1. Pick 5 hat ideas from this page.
2. Sketch each one on paper, Canva, Procreate, or any simple design app.
3. Show the 5 ideas to 10 to 20 local people.
4. Ask which one they would actually wear.
5. Write down the answers.

The goal is not to make the perfect hat today. The goal is to find out which designs people around you like before any money gets spent.

## Simple Design Rules

- Keep the logo original.
- Use clean colours.
- Make the design wearable with gym clothes, football training clothes, running clothes, hoodies, and tracksuits.
- Start with small logos before trying big logos.
- Do not copy Nike, Adidas, football clubs, schools, teams, characters, or luxury brands.

## Design 1: Black Everyday Training Cap

**Hat colour:** Black.

**Logo idea:** Small white initials or a simple original symbol.

**Front design:** Small logo in the centre front.

**Side/back design:** Tiny wordmark on the back strap or left side.

**Target customer:** People who go to the gym, play football, or wear activewear casually.

**Why someone local might wear it:** Black goes with nearly everything and feels easy to wear without standing out too much.

**What to avoid:** Do not make the logo too big. Do not add lots of text.

## Design 2: Grey Runner Cap

**Hat colour:** Light grey or medium grey.

**Logo idea:** Small dark logo that looks fast and simple, such as an original line mark or initials.

**Front design:** Small logo on the front left or centre.

**Side/back design:** Optional small reflective-style detail on the side, but keep it simple.

**Target customer:** Runners, walkers, park runners, and people who train outside.

**Why someone local might wear it:** Grey feels sporty, clean, and less hot-looking than black for outdoor training.

**What to avoid:** Do not use too many colours. Do not make it look like a school PE cap.

## Design 3: Navy Football Training Cap

**Hat colour:** Navy.

**Logo idea:** White or light grey initials, badge shape, or simple original mark.

**Front design:** Small badge-style logo on the front.

**Side/back design:** Optional small text on the back, such as the brand name only.

**Target customer:** Football players, coaches, and people who wear football training outfits.

**Why someone local might wear it:** Navy looks sporty and serious, and it works well with lots of team training colours.

**What to avoid:** Do not copy any football club badge, colours, slogans, or shirt designs.

## Design 4: White Summer Active Cap

**Hat colour:** White or off-white.

**Logo idea:** Small black, navy, or dark green logo.

**Front design:** Very clean front with one small logo.

**Side/back design:** No side design, or a tiny back logo only.

**Target customer:** People who want a clean summer cap for walking, gym outfits, running, or casual outfits.

**Why someone local might wear it:** White feels fresh and works well in warmer weather.

**What to avoid:** Do not make it look too plain or cheap. The logo placement needs to look neat.

## Design 5: Dark Green Local Active Cap

**Hat colour:** Dark green.

**Logo idea:** Cream or white original symbol inspired by movement, hills, roads, or a local feeling without copying any official place logo.

**Front design:** Small symbol on the front with no extra words.

**Side/back design:** Optional tiny brand name on the back.

**Target customer:** People who like something a bit different but still wearable.

**Why someone local might wear it:** Dark green feels sporty, outdoorsy, and more unique than plain black.

**What to avoid:** Do not make it too complicated. Do not use official council, school, team, or landmark logos.

## Best First Move

Start with the black, grey, and navy ideas first. They are the safest colours because more people are likely to wear them.

Only use the white or dark green ideas if people say they want something cleaner, brighter, or more different.
