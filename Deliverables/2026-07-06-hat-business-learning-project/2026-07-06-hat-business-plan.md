# Hat Business Plan

## Business Direction

Build a real local activewear hat brand.

The brand should make sporty, clean, wearable hats for people nearby who are into:

- Gym.
- Running.
- Football.
- Sports clubs.
- Casual activewear.
- Local lifestyle and streetwear.
- Teen and young adult fashion.

This is not a pretend project. The goal is to test whether local people would actually buy the hats, while keeping the first version small and sensible.

## Simple Goal

Create and sell a small first version of an activewear cap.

The first goal is to prove demand before spending too much money.

You are trying to learn:

- What local sporty people would actually wear.
- Which designs feel clean and not childish.
- What colours people prefer.
- What price feels fair.
- Which supplier can make a good activewear-style cap.
- Whether pre-orders, samples, or a small first batch makes the most sense.

## Brand Positioning

The brand should feel:

- Local.
- Sporty.
- Clean.
- Wearable.
- Confident.
- Modern.

It should not feel like:

- A school project.
- A joke brand.
- Too childish.
- Too busy.
- A copy of another brand.

Simple positioning sentence:

```text
A local activewear hat brand for gym, running, football, and everyday sporty style.
```

## Target Customer

Start local. Do not try to sell to everyone online yet.

Possible first customers:

- People at local gyms.
- Runners.
- Football players.
- Sports club members.
- Friends and family.
- Teens and young adults who wear activewear casually.
- People who like local brands and clean streetwear.

## Pricing Basics

Do not guess your selling price. Work it out with your dad using real numbers.

Collect:

- Blank cap cost.
- Embroidery or print cost.
- Sample cost.
- Shipping cost.
- Packaging cost.
- Website or marketplace fees.
- Payment fees.
- Similar activewear hat prices.

Simple formula:

```text
total cost per hat + profit = selling price
```

Example only:

```text
cap cost + decoration + packaging + fees + profit = selling price
```

Do not use this as the final price until you have real supplier numbers.

## Supplier And Manufacturing Options

### Option 1: Print-on-demand

A company makes and ships each hat after someone orders.

Good for:

- Testing with lower upfront cost.
- Avoiding large stock.

Check:

- Do they offer activewear or performance caps?
- Is the cap breathable and lightweight?
- Can you order a sample?
- Is embroidery or print available?
- What is the total cost with shipping?

### Option 2: Local embroidery or print shop

You buy activewear caps or source them through the shop, then they add the logo.

Good for:

- Checking quality in person.
- Building a local supplier relationship.
- Small first batches.

Check:

- Do they offer performance caps?
- What is the minimum order?
- What is the setup fee?
- Can they do small logos cleanly?
- How long does production take?

### Option 3: Wholesale activewear cap supplier

You and your dad find blank activewear caps, then arrange branding.

Good for:

- More control over product quality.
- Better cost if ordering more later.

Check:

- Minimum order.
- Sample availability.
- Colours.
- Fit.
- Material.
- Returns.

## Local Demand Research

Ask real local people before ordering too much.

Questions:

- Would you wear this hat?
- What colour would you buy?
- Would you prefer a small logo or bold logo?
- Would you wear it for gym, running, football, or casual outfits?
- What price would feel fair?
- Would you buy from a local young founder if the design was good?
- What would stop you from buying it?
- Which design feels most wearable?
- Does this feel like a real activewear brand?

Track answers in [[2026-07-06-product-research]].

## First Version Of The Business

Recommended first test:

- Pick 3 possible local sporty brand names.
- Create 5 activewear hat design ideas.
- Ask 10 to 20 local people which designs they would actually wear.
- Research 3 suppliers for activewear or performance caps.
- Work out sample costs with Dad.
- Decide whether to test with pre-orders, samples, or a small first batch.

## Success For The First Test

The first test succeeds if you know:

- Which design people actually like.
- Which colours are most popular.
- Whether people would pay a realistic price.
- Whether the cap quality is good enough.
- Whether local people take the brand seriously.
- Whether the next step should be pre-orders, samples, or a small first batch.
