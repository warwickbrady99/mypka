# Next Actions Checklist

Work through this in order. Keep it simple and local.

## Step 1: Pick 3 Possible Local Sporty Brand Names

- [ ] Read [[2026-07-06-brand-niche-ideas]].
- [ ] Write 3 possible local sporty brand names.
- [ ] Check that they do not sound childish.
- [ ] Check that they are not copying another brand.
- [ ] Ask 5 people which name sounds most wearable.

## Step 2: Create 5 Activewear Hat Design Ideas

- [ ] Choose simple colours: black, white, grey, navy, or dark green.
- [ ] Make 5 design ideas.
- [ ] Try small logo versions first.
- [ ] Make sure each design could work for gym, running, football, or casual outfits.
- [ ] Save notes on why each design might work.

## Step 3: Ask 10 To 20 Local People

- [ ] Ask friends, family, gym people, runners, football players, or sporty local people.
- [ ] Ask: "Would you wear this hat?"
- [ ] Ask: "What colour would you buy?"
- [ ] Ask: "Would you prefer a small logo or bold logo?"
- [ ] Ask: "Would you wear it for gym, running, football, or casual?"
- [ ] Ask: "What price would feel fair?"
- [ ] Ask: "Would you buy from a local young founder if the design was good?"
- [ ] Record answers in [[2026-07-06-product-research]].

## Step 4: Research 3 Activewear Cap Suppliers

- [ ] Find 1 print-on-demand option with performance or activewear caps.
- [ ] Find 1 local print or embroidery shop.
- [ ] Find 1 wholesale blank cap supplier.
- [ ] Check whether samples are available.
- [ ] Check minimum order quantities.
- [ ] Check whether they offer lightweight or breathable caps.
- [ ] Check embroidery and print options.

## Step 5: Work Out Sample Costs With Dad

- [ ] Show Dad the supplier options.
- [ ] Work out sample cost.
- [ ] Work out shipping cost.
- [ ] Work out decoration cost.
- [ ] Work out packaging cost.
- [ ] Check any platform or payment fees.
- [ ] Decide the maximum amount you are comfortable spending on the first test.

## Step 6: Choose The First Test Method

- [ ] Decide with Dad whether to use pre-orders.
- [ ] Decide with Dad whether to order samples first.
- [ ] Decide with Dad whether to make a small first batch.
- [ ] Pick only 1 to 3 designs for the first test.
- [ ] Choose the first colour or colours.
- [ ] Write the first test plan before spending money.

## Dad / Adult Support Checklist

- [ ] Dad approves any supplier account.
- [ ] Dad approves any payment setup.
- [ ] Dad approves any official business, legal, or tax setup.
- [ ] Dad approves any product order.
- [ ] Dad helps check customer data and privacy.
